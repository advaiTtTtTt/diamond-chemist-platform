🌐 Part 2 — Website Requirements (You Build This)
Razorpay requires the following pages to be live on the website before approval — they run automated checks on your URL during submission: Razorpay
MANDATORY PAGES — all must be live before KYC submission:

  [ ] Privacy Policy
      — what data you collect, why, how it's used
      — must mention phone numbers, addresses, GPS data

  [ ] Terms & Conditions
      — rules of using the platform
      — liability, age restrictions, acceptable use

  [ ] Refund & Cancellation Policy
      — must state processing timelines explicitly
        e.g. "Refunds processed within 5–7 business days"
      — for print jobs: what happens if print fails
      — for medicines: what happens if item unavailable

  [ ] Shipping / Delivery Policy
      — delivery area (50 metres)
      — estimated delivery time
      — what happens if delivery fails

  [ ] Contact Us page
      — working phone number with business hours
      — working email address
      — physical shop address

  [ ] Pricing page (or pricing visible on product pages)
      — all product prices clearly displayed
      — print pricing clearly displayed
      — no hidden charges
Both the Privacy Policy and Refund Policy must be linked in the footer of every page — not just reachable from a menu. Razorpay

🖥️ Part 3 — Website Technical Requirements (You Verify This)
  [ ] Website must be on a live domain (not localhost, 
      not a preview URL like vercel.app)
      — get diamondchemist.in or similar before submitting

  [ ] SSL certificate active (HTTPS, not HTTP)
      — Vercel handles this automatically on custom domains

  [ ] Checkout flow must be complete and testable
      — Razorpay reviewer will go through the 
        entire purchase journey themselves

  [ ] At least 5 live product listings visible
      — the 15 demo products satisfy this easily

  [ ] Shop name, logo, and address visible on the site
      — must match the business registration documents

  [ ] No broken links, no "coming soon" on critical pages
      — remove or hide incomplete features before submission
